Humor can provide a method of coping with a variety of stressful situations. Training of humor-related skills has proven effective in clinical samples, although humor training in patients with schizophrenia is relatively rare.

<offsets xml_i="3501" xml_f="3508" txt_i="259" txt_f="266">Methods</offsets>

In the present study, patients with schizophrenia have been randomly assigned to either a training of humor abilities or a training of social skills. Training effects on measures of psychopathology, psychosocial functioning and stress were compared between groups.

<offsets xml_i="3813" xml_f="3820" txt_i="533" txt_f="540">Results</offsets>

Preliminary analyses revealed that level of negative symptoms, stress and psychosocial dysfunction were significantly reduced in the humor group over the course of the training.

<offsets xml_i="4038" xml_f="4048" txt_i="720" txt_f="730">Discussion</offsets>

These results suggest that humor training may improve important clinical and functional outcomes in patients with schizophrenia.
