Task

This task is about identifying and grouping mentions of medical concepts in abstracts from Randomized Controlled Trials.

Interventions or comparators (collectively called treatments) associated with different arms of the trial, or the measured outcomes are often mentioned several times throughout the abstract. The goal of this task is to highlight all occurences of one of the trial's treatments and outcomes, and group together repeated mentions of the same concept.

The annotation process has three components to it:

  • Highlight every occurence of a studied treatment or outcome.
  • For every different treatment or outcome you find in the article, assign its mentions to a different group.
  • Go through the previously extracted annotations for the article and assign them to the relevant groups.

Details: Highlighting

As you read through the article, highlight every snippet of text you find that represents one of the studied treatments or outcomes.

  • We are only interested in the treatments and outcomes that are actively evaluated in this particular trial.
  • Keep the highlighted text as short as possible while including any details that are specific to that snippet.
  • Try to include details that differentiate the snippet from other similar but clinically different snippets in the document (such as any dosages that go with the treatments, and any endpoints that go with the outcomes).
  • If multiple distinct concepts are listed in a row (such as a list of outcomes), highlight them individually.
  • For complex interventions that involve multiple treatments, include as much of the full intervention strategy as you can in the highlighted snippet.

Details: Grouping

After highlighting each snippet, group it together with other snippets you've already highlighted that are clinically equivalent in the context of this trial. In other words, all of the snippets within a single group should be roughly interchangeable in this abstract. If you don't already have a group that fits the highlighted snippet, create a new group for it.

  • When deciding how to group snippets, ignore any syntactic differences such as verb tense or pluralization.
  • If a specific treatment or outcome is referred to by a more general term, include it in the same group as long is it is unambiguous in the context of the abstract. However if a general term is used to refer to multiple arms of a trial or to a group of measured outcomes, assign the general term to a new group.
  • When creating a group, you can pick any name you like - the names are only to help faciliate the annotation process.

Interface

Overview

The interface for this task is designed for you to highlight as you read through the article, and immediately assign the highlights to groups that you create.

The annotation interface containes three main components:

  • Article Tabs: each full-text article is broken up in to sections. For this task, only the Abstract tab will be available.
  • Tab Content: this displays the text content of the current section. This is where you will highlight the relevant text snippets.
  • Created Groups: creating a new group will add a tab here that you can use to navigate between them.
  • Highlighted snippets: each snippet you add to the selected group will be collected in this area for you to reference.

Automatic Suggestions

Due to the repetitive nature of this task, we have implemented a feature to help reduce the amount of simple highlighting you need to do. When you highlight a snippet and add it to a group, the interface will automatically pre-highlight all other instances of that snippet throughout the article. These suggested snippets are not added until you confirm them by hovering over them using the popup menu to either confirm (‚úì) or discard (êÑÇ) each snippet.

Please be careful when using this feature, and discard and re-highlight any instances where the suggested span is not correct. For example if you highlight "heel pain", you may then encounter a sentence that includes the suggested snippet "plantar heel pain". In this situation you would need to discard the suggestion so that you can highlight the complete outcome, "plantar heel pain".

Example

Here is an example of what the interface looks like during the annotation process. You can see how the different outcomes have been divided in to different groups.

  1. The first mention of 'first-step' pain includes extra detail about how the outcome is specified.
  2. Foot pain is similar to 'first-step' pain, but is not interchangeable in this trial and is assigned to a new group.
  3. The second mention of 'first-step' pain includes a specific endpoint, but since only one endpoint is measured in this trial this mention is semantically equivalent to the first.
  4. The first treatment mentioned is a complex intervention, so we highlight the entire snippet as a single mention.
  5. Subsequent references to the low-Dye taping and sham ultrasound intervention don't explicitly include the complete intervention, but in the context of this article they refer to the same intervention and are assigned to the same group.

Existing Annotations

Previous annotators have provided details about the article's interventions, comparators, and outcomes. The last tab of the article contains these annotations.

Assign each of these snippets to the relevant group by clicking on it and then adding it. Once you have assigned all of the provided annotations, you may submit the article.

Use the following keybindings to facilitate annotation:

  • s: add highlighted/selected snippet to current group
  • e/<del>: remove selected snippet
  • g: create new group
  • d: delete current group
  • t: show Treatment groups
  • o: show Outcome groups
  • [1-9]: show the numbered group

Double-click on a group to rename it, or shift + double-click a group to create a new group starting from the same name.
